title: "Manage projects"
description: "Manage rendered videos, download assets, and share videos to connected platforms."
---

In **Projects**, you can manage all videos you have created in YupVid.

Use this page to review rendered videos, download assets, and share finished videos directly to connected platforms.

## Project list

Each project shows the main information you need:

- A video preview.
- Video title.
- Description or short content summary.
- Render status, such as **done**.
- Render completion time.
- **Open in Studio** button.
- **Downloads** section.
- **Publish to platforms** section.

You can use this page as the final output hub for your rendered videos.

## Review a rendered video

Each project includes a video preview on the left.

Play the video before downloading or publishing it. Check that:

- The content is correct.
- Subtitles look right.
- The voice sounds correct.
- The thumbnail and visuals are appropriate.
- The video is ready to publish.

## Edit a video again

If you need to change a video, click **Open in Studio**.

YupVid opens the project in Studio so you can edit:

- Script
- Media
- Voice
- Subtitles
- Theme
- Effect
- Watermark
- Ending

After editing, render the project again to create a new video file.

## Download video assets

In **Downloads**, you can download the project assets:

| Button | What it downloads |
| --- | --- |
| **MP4** | The final video file for manual posting or storage |
| **Thumbnail** | The video thumbnail image |
| **Voice** | The generated voice or audio file |
| **Subtitles** | The subtitle file |

If you want to post to a platform that is not connected to YupVid, download the **MP4** and upload it manually.

## Share videos to platforms

**Publish to platforms** is where you share the video.

YupVid shows the connected accounts, pages, or channels available for that project. Each platform card usually includes:

- Account avatar or platform icon.
- Account, page, or channel name.
- Platform name, such as **Facebook Reel**, **YouTube Short**, **TikTok**, **Threads**, **Instagram**, **LinkedIn**, or **X (Twitter)**.
- Publishing status.
- **Upload** or **View** button.

You can publish the same video to multiple platforms from the same project.

## Publishing statuses

Each platform card shows the current status of the video.

| Status | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Ready to upload** | The video is ready to publish to that platform |
| **Published** | The video has been published successfully |

If the status is **Ready to upload**, click **Upload** to send the video to that platform.

If the status is **Published**, click **View** to open the published video.

## How to publish a video

1. Open **Projects**.
2. Find the project you want to share.
3. Review the video preview.
4. In **Publish to platforms**, choose the account or platform.
5. Click **Upload**.
6. Wait for YupVid to upload the video.
7. When publishing succeeds, the status changes to **Published**.
8. Click **View** to open the published video.

## Publish to multiple accounts

If you connected multiple accounts or pages, YupVid shows each account in the platform list.

You can publish one video to different accounts, such as:

- A Facebook page.
- A YouTube channel.
- A TikTok account.
- A Threads account.
- An Instagram account.
- A LinkedIn account.
- An X account.

This lets you distribute the same video across several channels without downloading the MP4 and uploading it manually to each site.

## When to use Upload

Use **Upload** when the video is ready to publish to a platform.

Before uploading, check that:

- The video has finished rendering.
- The content does not need more edits.
- The platform is connected to the correct account.
- Any publishing settings are correct.

## When to use View

Use **View** when the video has been published and you want to open the post on the platform.

The **View** button helps you verify the published post after sharing.

## YouTube private note

For YouTube, videos are uploaded as **private**.

After YupVid uploads the video to YouTube, open **YouTube Studio** to review it and publish it publicly if you want it to be visible.
